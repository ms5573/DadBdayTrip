Introduction

Option 1: Tokyo Route

Get ready for an 18-day Japan adventure in mid-to-late April 2026 that starts in Tokyo and loops through serene onsens, historic shrines, and vibrant cultural hubs. This itinerary is designed to keep things comfortable and fun without being too hectic.

What's on the Agenda?

  • Tokyo: Explore Senso-ji Temple in Asakusa, soak up neon in Shibuya and Ginza, and indulge in street food at Solamachi.
  • Hakone: Relax in onsen baths, cruise Lake Ashi on a pirate ship and ride the ropeway over Owakudani with stunning views of Mount Fuji.
  • Kyoto: Wander the Arashiyama Bamboo Grove, stroll through Fushimi Inari's torii tunnels, explore Gion's teahouses and browse Nishiki Market.
  • Hiroshima & Miyajima: Visit Peace Memorial Park, then ferry to Miyajima Island to see the iconic floating torii gate and meet the friendly deer.
  • Kanazawa & Back to Tokyo: Discover Kenroku-en Garden and Nagamachi's samurai quarter before returning to Tokyo.

Travel Tips

Essential information for our Japan journey, compiled to help ensure a smooth and enjoyable experience.

Transportation

The Japan Rail Pass is an excellent investment for long-distance travel. For city travel, consider SUICA/PASMO IC cards which work on most public transit systems.

Communication

Pocket WiFi or a Japanese SIM card will help with navigation. Many Japanese speak limited English, so a translation app can be useful.

Etiquette

Remove shoes when entering homes, some restaurants, and ryokans. Bow when greeting. Avoid tipping - it's not customary in Japan.

Money

Japan is still largely cash-based. Exchange money at the airport or withdraw from 7-Eleven ATMs, which accept international cards.
