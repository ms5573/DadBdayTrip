Introduction

Welcome to the digital journey of Robert Smith's 70th birthday trip to Japan. In April 2025, Robert will embark on a once-in-a-lifetime adventure through the Land of the Rising Sun, exploring its rich culture, stunning landscapes, and remarkable cuisine. This website chronicles the planning and eventually the memories of this special celebration.

Japan has always held a special place in Robert's heart, with its unique blend of ancient traditions and cutting-edge modernity. From the serene temples of Kyoto to the bustling streets of Tokyo, this journey will take him through the many facets of Japanese culture, cuisine, and natural beauty.

This 18-day adventure has been meticulously planned to coincide with cherry blossom season, allowing Robert to experience the ephemeral beauty of sakura - a metaphor for life's precious moments that are all the more beautiful for their transience.

Travel Tips

Essential information for our Japan journey, compiled to help ensure a smooth and enjoyable experience.

Transportation

The Japan Rail Pass is an excellent investment for long-distance travel. For city travel, consider SUICA/PASMO IC cards which work on most public transit systems.

Communication

Pocket WiFi or a Japanese SIM card will help with navigation. Many Japanese speak limited English, so a translation app can be useful.

Etiquette

Remove shoes when entering homes, some restaurants, and ryokans. Bow when greeting. Avoid tipping - it's not customary in Japan.

Money

Japan is still largely cash-based. Exchange money at the airport or withdraw from 7-Eleven ATMs, which accept international cards.
